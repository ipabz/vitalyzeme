• Inflammation
  • Inflammation is the body’s main line of defense and helps repair damaged tissue
  • Inflammation is the body’s response to
  • stress
  • injury
  • illness
  • infection
  • For example, if you get bitten by a snake, you need inflammation to
  • stop bleeding
  • fight infection
  • escape to preserve your life
  • In today’s world however, Inflammation is one of the deadliest causes of disease. Why?
  • Inflammation was developed as an evolutionary defense against physical and environmental threats
  • It helped us become resistant to insulin, to help us store fat and fight famine
  • Inflammation also existed when we primarily lived on whole foods, like grains and nuts, and protein
  • In today’s world, we no longer have many physical threats, our food is no longer whole foods and protein, and technology makes us sedentary, where synthetic toxins pollute our body, and our natural rhythms no longer tell us when to sleep or wake.
  • Today, too much inflammation causes:
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Systemic lupus erythematosis (SLE)
  • Asthma
  • Allergic diseases
  • Earlier and more severe strokes, heart attacks, cancer, diabetes, Alzheimers - Inflammation Kills
