• Hormones
  • Hormones are chemical messengers
  • The body’s wireless communication system
  • Turns cell functions on and off
  • Regulates Gene Expression
  • Hormones are responsible for
  • Signalling to cells to Prepare, Grow and Die (Apoptosis)

  • Helping to regulate Metabolism
  • Mood

  • Optimal Hormone Levels
  • Occur during our 20s and 30s
  • Gradually and subtly decline
  • May be responsible for the lack of disease during these years of our lives
  • Hormone levels decrease as we age
  • Our biological age is closely tied to the health and activity of your endocrine system
  • One theory is that hormones are nature’s way of keeping people around long enough to reproduce, and then ‘cut us off’ as we are no longer useful to the tribe
  • Sex Hormone Decline in men
  • Hormonal decline starts in men in the early 30s
  • Eventually leads to Testosterone Insufficiency, or Andropause
  • Other names include Hypogonadism, Male Menopause, Androgen Deficiency Syndrome of Aging Males
  • By the age of 80, testosterone levels are only 1/5th of the level in his/her 30s
  • Today, low hormone states happen more commonly
  • up to 40% of men over 45 have low levels of testosterone
  • Also occurs in those with high levels of body fat
  • Fat contains an enzyme called ‘aromatase’, which converts testosterone into estradiol - this is known as metabolic andropause
  • This decreases the level of testosterone available in the body and causes the same symptoms
